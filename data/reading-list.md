## Books

### Gay Sydney: A History
- **Author:** Wotherspoon, Garry
- **Year:** 2016
- **Publisher:** New South Printing, Sydney
- **Type:** book
- **Found via:** SCOI report citations
- **Status:** referenced (in REFERENCES.md as secondary source — not yet read in full)
- **Relevance:** Leading academic history of queer Sydney; key source for venue and beat geography across the project period. Cited by Sackar J in the SCOI report itself.

### Voices from 1978
- **Author:** First Mardi Gras 78ers
- **Type:** booklet
- **URL:** https://firstmardigras.com.au (available for purchase)
- **Found via:** PHG reading list, 2026-07-18
- **Status:** to-read
- **Relevance:** First-person accounts of the 1978 Mardi Gras and police confrontation — directly relevant to the Oxford Street / Taylor Square context and police conduct in that period. "The events of 1978, told in the words of those who were there."

## Online Projects & Exhibitions

### A City Responds to Crisis
- **URL:** https://acityrespondstocrisis.com
- **Type:** online exhibition
- **Found via:** PHG reading list, 2026-07-18
- **Status:** to-read
- **Relevance:** Documents the role of volunteers in Sydney during the HIV/AIDS epidemic (1980s–90s). Oral histories and archival material. HIV/AIDS deaths are in scope for the project (several SCOI cases intersect with this era). May contain oral history metadata or leads.

### Small Town Queer (Tweed Regional Museum)
- **URL:** https://museum.tweed.nsw.gov.au/explore/people-places/small-town-queer
- **Type:** online exhibition
- **Found via:** PHG reading list (via Northern Rivers/Tropical Fruits Queer History Project), 2026-07-18
- **Status:** to-read
- **Relevance:** Regional queer history exhibition — useful as a model for how museums are approaching this material. Northern Rivers context may not be directly relevant to Sydney cases but the methodology is.

### Living Histories — Hunter (University of Newcastle)
- **URL:** https://livinghistories.newcastle.edu.au/nodes/view/59991
- **Type:** oral history collection (online)
- **Found via:** PHG reading list (via Hunter Rainbow History Group), 2026-07-18
- **Status:** to-read
- **Relevance:** LGBTIQ oral histories from Newcastle/Hunter region. Useful as a model for oral history metadata and access — and Hunter-region context may be relevant to some cases. Worth understanding their access model.

## Repositories & Archives to Investigate

*These are organisations that hold relevant collections — they may need a `source-collections/` entry once investigated.*

### Australian Queer Archives (AQuA)
- **URL:** https://queerarchives.org.au
- **Type:** archive
- **Found via:** PHG reading list, 2026-07-18
- **Status:** to-investigate
- **Source collections entry:** `data/sydney/source-collections/aqua.md` *(to create)*
- **Relevance:** Largest LGBTIQ historical archive in Australia. Based in Melbourne but accessible via State Library NSW. National scope — will hold material relevant to Sydney cases, law reform period, community organisations. Already in ArchiveSource.institution enum as `aqua`.
- **Notes:** PHG describes them as "the biggest repository of historical materials about LGBTIQ experience in Australia. Established 1978."

### Hunter Rainbow History Group
- **URL:** https://www.hunterqhistory.org.au (check current URL)
- **Type:** oral history project / community archive
- **Found via:** PHG reading list, 2026-07-18
- **Status:** to-investigate
- **Relevance:** Recording and collecting LGBTIQ stories from Newcastle and Hunter. Partnered with University of Newcastle for oral history hosting. Model for community-led history methodology. May have contacts or context relevant to regional cases.

### Northern Rivers / Tropical Fruits Queer History Project

- **Type:** community archive
- **Found via:** PHG reading list, 2026-07-18
- **Status:** to-investigate
- **Relevance:** Northern NSW LGBTIQ history project. Regional scope — probably not directly relevant to Sydney cases, but methodology and network contacts may be useful.
