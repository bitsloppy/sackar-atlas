Robert Knight and Robyn Conlan are the parents of Simon, a gay man who has been missing since July 2005.

When Robert and Robyn first reported their son missing to police, Robyn told them that Simon identified as gay. The response: "gays go missing all the time … he'll turn up."

Investigations began — but not before an initial refusal by the Unsolved Homicide Team to investigate, on the grounds that the case had not been referred by the Coroner. Investigations eventually progressed, but years later, nothing had eventuated. A $100,000 police reward was announced in 2008.

In February 2019, Robert and Robyn were told that Redfern Police Area Command would be in contact to arrange a meeting, after Peter Rolfe from *Support After Murder* had written to the Police Commissioner on their behalf highlighting matters they did not believe had been properly investigated. Almost three weeks passed without contact. A second letter was needed — expressing concern that the inaction was because Simon is gay. A response came almost immediately.

In April 2019, Robert and Robyn met with officers at Redfern Police Station. They brought a 2013 magazine article in which the Crime Coordinator for Redfern PAC was reported to have said that Simon was well known in the Newtown area and was believed to be alive and well — Redfern PAC did not have a copy of the article. They were told that Simon's case would be transferred to Kings Cross PAC for an independent review.

Four months after that meeting, Robert and Robyn had still heard nothing. They followed up themselves. In September 2019, they finally met at Redfern Police Station to discuss the review report. They asked if they could take a copy; they were refused — it was confidential. Instead, they were given fifteen minutes to read it in the room. Sensing the attending officer becoming impatient, they left.

In November 2020 — fifteen years after Simon went missing — the police reward was increased to $250,000. A coronial inquest had been conducted. Robert and Robyn gave evidence to the NSW Parliamentary Committee inquiry still wondering, as they put it, "if it is because he is gay."
