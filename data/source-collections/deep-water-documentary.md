**Deep Water: The Real Story** is a feature-length documentary (1h 27m) directed by Amanda Blue for Blackfella Films, produced for SBS with major funding from Screen Australia. It premiered Sunday 16 October 2016 at 8.30pm on SBS.

The film investigates the spate of violence and crime on Sydney's coastal cliffs during the 1980s and 1990s — gang assaults, mysterious deaths officially recorded as "suicide", "disappearance", and "misadventure". Individual stories are woven together through first-person interviews and detailed re-enactments.

Blackfella Films is the production company behind *First Contact*, *Redfern Now*, and *First Australians*.

## Cases and testimony covered

- **Ross Warren** — disappeared July 1989, Marks Park/Tamarama
- **John Russell** — murdered November 1989, Bondi cliffs; body found next morning at base of cliffs; inquest lasted seven minutes, finding "death by misadventure"
- **David McMahon** — survivor; attacked December 1989, dragged down three flights of stairs; heard attacker say *"let's take him up and throw him off where we threw the other dude"*
- Gilles Mattaini — named in SBS press materials; French national found at base of cliffs

## Key sources in the documentary

The film interviews people who had not previously spoken publicly, including:
- Survivors of attacks (including David McMahon)
- Witnesses
- Families of victims
- Ex-police from original investigations
- Investigative journalists
- Forensic pathologists
- **Ex-Deputy State Coroner Jacqueline Milledge** — who led the 2005 inquest and found Warren and Russell were murdered

## Relationship to this project

Deep Water: The Real Story predates the SCOI (2023) by seven years and is one of the most substantial documentary treatments of the Bondi cliffs cases. The interviews — particularly with Milledge, the ex-Deputy State Coroner, and with McMahon — are primary-quality testimony captured in documentary form.

The film raises the central accountability question that also structures this project: *"Where does the responsibility lie for these crimes — the perpetrators, the police, or a wider society in the grips of homophobia, misinformation and fear?"*

## Note on the companion drama

A companion dramatic series, *Deep Water* (2016, SBS), starring Yael Stone and Noah Taylor, fictionalised the same history. That series is a separate work and should not be cited as a factual source. This record covers the documentary only.
