Pride History Group is Sydney's foremost LGBTIQ oral history repository, holding
over 100 recorded interviews spanning the 1960s–2000s. Their "100 Voices" collection
covers key themes: spaces and beats, Mardi Gras origins, activism and law reform,
HIV/AIDS, arts and culture, and diverse community identities.

The collection is a primary source for understanding the lived context of the
deaths documented in this project — who the community was, what the police
relationship was like, what the beats meant, and how people organised.

## Access notes

- Publicly available interviews can be streamed at pridehistory.org.au/oral-histories
- Interviews marked `*` are held by PHG but restricted — not publicly accessible
- All use beyond personal/research requires contacting PHG

## What exists in the collection

Themes relevant to this project:
- **Spaces** — beats, pubs, venues; includes material on areas where deaths occurred
- **Activism** — law reform campaigns, police accountability organising
- **Mardi Gras** — 1978 riot and aftermath; police conduct in that period
