Feature documentary on the Bondi cliffs gay hate crimes. Directed by Amanda Blue, produced by Blackfella Films for SBS. Premiered 16 October 2016, 1h 27m.

## Key significance

Rated **primary-source-quality** for its interviews with:

- **Deputy State Coroner Jacqueline Milledge** — who presided over the 2005 inquest and found probable gay hate motivations for the Bondi cliffs deaths. Her interview here is a key record of her reasoning and findings.
- **David McMahon** — survivor of a December 1989 attack at Marks Park. His testimony, including the *"let's take him up and throw him off where we threw the other dude"* quote, is foundational to understanding the premeditated, organised nature of the attacks.
- Ex-police
- Journalists who covered the original cases
- Families of victims

## Research notes

This documentary predates the SCOI (2022–2023) by six years. It provides a baseline account of the Bondi cliffs cases with the knowledge available in 2016, including the 2005 Milledge inquest findings but before the full SCOI investigation.

The Milledge interview is particularly significant: she is the primary decision-maker in the 2005 inquest and her on-camera account of what she found and why is primary oral history.
