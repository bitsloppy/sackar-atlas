Jim Lees served as Commissioner of Police from October 1979 to December 1981. His tenure bridged the period between the first Mardi Gras crackdown of 1978 and the passage of the Wran government's anti-discrimination protections. Male homosexual acts remained criminal offences throughout his time as Commissioner, and this period saw the early emergence of anti-gay violence in Sydney's inner-city precincts that would intensify dramatically over the following decade.
