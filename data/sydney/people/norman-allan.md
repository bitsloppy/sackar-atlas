Norman Allan served as Commissioner of Police from February 1962 to November 1972. His decade-long tenure spanned the height of the criminal law era in New South Wales, during which male homosexual acts remained offences carrying penalties of up to fourteen years imprisonment. This was also the period in which the Beats and Kings Cross gay subculture were emerging in Sydney, with police regularly using consorting and vagrancy laws to surveil and arrest gay men.
