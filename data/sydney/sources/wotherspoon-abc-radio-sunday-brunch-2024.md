ABC Radio Sydney *Sydney Weekend Mornings* Sunday Brunch segment. Historian and 78er Garry Wotherspoon interviewed at his Oxford Street home the morning after Mardi Gras 2024.

## Significance

Rated **primary-source-quality**: Wotherspoon is a first-hand witness to the history of gay Sydney from the 1970s onwards. As a 78er (participant in the first 1978 Mardi Gras), a leading queer historian, and a long-term Oxford Street resident, his recollections are primary-quality oral history.

## Key content

- Oxford Street as "the ghetto" — its formation, character, and significance through the HIV/AIDS era
- The social geography of gay Sydney: beats, venues, community spaces
- The post-Mardi Gras morning atmosphere on Oxford Street
- His own experience as a witness to multiple decades of queer history

This segment should be cross-referenced with Wotherspoon's ABC News quote in the Oxford Street heritage listing article (March 2025) and his appearances in the Deep Water documentary (2016). Together these provide a longitudinal oral history record.
