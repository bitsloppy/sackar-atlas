Episode 4 of the *Bondi Badlands* podcast. Covers the 2005 coronial inquest into the Bondi cliffs deaths, Operation Taradale, and survivor David McMahon.

## Key significance

This episode is rated **primary-source-quality** for its interviews with:

- **Det. Sgt. Stephen Page** — led Operation Taradale, the cold-case reinvestigation; instrumental in the 2005 inquest. Now retired.
- **David McMahon** — survived an attack at Marks Park in December 1989. His testimony at inquest included the phrase: *"Let's take him up and throw him off where we threw the other dude"* — establishing a pattern of premeditated group attacks on the headland.

Deputy State Coroner Jacqueline Milledge's findings are covered: she condemned the original police investigations as "lacklustre" and "shameful", and found probable gay hate motivations for the Bondi cliffs deaths.

The episode also covers the role of prison phone taps in identifying witnesses and the emergence of new evidence more than a decade after the original crimes.
