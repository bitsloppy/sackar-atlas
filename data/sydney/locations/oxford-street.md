Stub — Oxford Street, Darlinghurst, is the historic heart of LGBTIQ Sydney. The
strip between Taylor Square and Paddington Town Hall concentrated bars, clubs,
bookshops, and community organisations from the late 1970s onward — becoming
the social and political centre of gay and lesbian Sydney through the AIDS crisis,
the hate crime years, and the law reform campaigns of the 1980s and 1990s.

The route of the annual Sydney Gay and Lesbian Mardi Gras parade runs along
Oxford Street; the first Mardi Gras march (28 June 1978) ended in police
violence and mass arrests at the top of Oxford Street near Hyde Park.

Oxford Street's significance to this project is primarily contextual: it was the
community hub from which the responses to anti-gay violence were organised, and
where many of the victims of hate crimes socialised before their deaths. It also
appears directly in some cases as a last-known location.

As of March 2025, a NSW Heritage Office assessment of Oxford Street's LGBTIQ
cultural heritage significance was underway — a direct result of advocacy
following the SCOI findings.
