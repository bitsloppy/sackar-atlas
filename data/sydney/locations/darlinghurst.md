Stub — The Oxford Street precinct in Darlinghurst is the cultural and social centre of queer Sydney. From the 1970s onward it developed as the heart of gay Sydney: venues, community organisations, activist groups, and later the annual Mardi Gras route.

**In this project's scope:**
- The first Sydney Gay and Lesbian Mardi Gras (1978) marched along Oxford Street; police baton-charged the crowd and arrested 53 people.
- Oxford Street venues (Oxford Hotel, Palms, Universal) were proposed for heritage listing in 2025 — responding in part to Justice Sackar's call in the SCOI report.
- In June 2026, NSW Police raided Oxford Street venues during Pride Month, prompting Garry Wotherspoon to resign from the NSW Police LGBTQIA+ Consultative Committee.

Most of the hate crime deaths documented in this project's scope occurred in the eastern suburbs — Marks Park, North Head, Shelley Headland — but Oxford Street is the cultural gravity well that these cases orbit.
