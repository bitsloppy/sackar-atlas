Stub — Marks Park is a coastal reserve on the southern headland of Bondi Beach,
above the cliffs between Bondi and Tamarama. From at least the late 1970s through
the 1990s, the park's scrubland and cliff-edge paths were a known gay beat. It was
the site of multiple deaths documented by the Sackar Inquiry, and is one of the
most significant locations in the project scope.

The "Bondi Boys" — a loosely affiliated group of young men who targeted gay men
at and around Marks Park — are linked to multiple cases. The area was also the
site of violence by the "Tamarama Three" and other perpetrators. ACON's *In Pursuit
of Truth & Justice* (2018) identifies the Marks Park/Bondi headland area as one
of the primary sites of gang-organised anti-gay violence in Sydney during the
1980s and 1990s.

A memorial plaque was installed by Waverley Council in 2003 — one of the earliest
formal public memorials to anti-gay hate crime victims in Australia.
