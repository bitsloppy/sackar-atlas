The Star Observer is Australia's longest-running LGBTIQ publication, founded in
1979. Its archive at archive.starobserver.com.au covers 1979–present: 1,652 issues
and over 61,000 pages of community journalism.

For this project, the Star Observer is the single most important contemporaneous
community press source. It was reporting on the Sydney LGBTIQ community from the
very beginning of the hate crime period documented by the SCOI, through the peak
violence years of the 1980s, and into the accountability and reform period of the
1990s–2000s. Where mainstream newspapers often ignored or minimised anti-gay
violence, the Star Observer named it, covered police failures, and reported
community responses.

## What to look for

### Case-level research
For each case in the project scope, search the Star Observer archive for:
- The victim's name (full name + any known aliases or name variants)
- Reporting of the death, inquest, police investigation
- Community responses — vigils, protests, calls for investigation
- Later revisiting: coronial updates, SCOI-era coverage

### Contextual research
- **Police accountability reporting**: Star Observer covered tensions between
  police and the LGBTIQ community extensively. Reports on raids, police conduct
  at beats, accountability campaigns
- **Beats geography**: venue and beat coverage locates the social context of
  specific locations
- **Law reform**: decriminalisation debates, hate crime legislation, coronial
  reform — links deaths to political response
- **CAMP Inc, Gay Solidarity Group, other organisations**: community organising
  responses to violence

### Key periods to prioritise
- **1979–1981**: project period begins; earliest deaths; community climate
- **1982–1989**: peak violence years; police conduct; Oxford St growth
- **1990–1995**: accountability campaigns; law reform; coronial pressure
- **2023**: SCOI coverage and community response

## Citation format (AGSM)
