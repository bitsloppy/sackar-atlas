# testimonies/

Survivor accounts, family witness statements, and community testimony formally
entered into the public record through parliamentary inquiries, the SCOI, oral
history programs, and media interviews.

## What belongs here

A testimony is a **person's full account** — which may document multiple incidents
across years, their experience of institutional responses, and ongoing impact.

This is distinct from the `cases/` collection, which records individual incidents
(primarily deaths, with some missing persons and assault-survived cases).

Key distinction: one person may give testimony covering three separate attacks
over twenty years (Stewart South). Each attack can be documented as an `incident`
within the single testimony record.

## Provenance

Every testimony must include a precise `source_reference` in AGSM author-date
format. These are public records — parliamentary submissions have submission
numbers, SCOI hearings have transcript references, oral histories have accession
numbers. Cite them.
