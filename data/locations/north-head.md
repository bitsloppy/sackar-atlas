Stub — North Head is the rugged headland at the northern entrance to Sydney
Harbour, within Sydney Harbour National Park. Like the nearby Shelley Headland,
it was a known gay beat from the 1970s through the 1990s — a place where men
went to meet other men, and where isolation made them targets for predatory violence.

The most prominent case at North Head is that of **Scott Johnson**, an American
mathematician whose naked body was found at the base of the cliffs at Blue Fish
Point on 10 December 1988. Three coronial inquests followed over almost thirty
years. The third inquest (2017) found he had fallen "as a result of actual or
threatened violence from unidentified persons who attacked him because they
perceived him to be homosexual."

North Head is distinct from — though geographically close to — the **Shelley
Headland** (see `shelley-headland.md`), where Mark Stewart (1976) and Paul Rath
(1977) died.
