Stub — Alan Rosendale is a survivor of an anti-gay attack at Moore Park in 1989.
He is a named case in ACON's *In Pursuit of Truth & Justice* (2018) and in
*Report 58* (NSW Legislative Council Standing Committee on Social Issues, 2021).
His case is documented as `case_outcome: assault-survived`.

An eyewitness, Paul Simes, recorded the attackers' vehicle registration at the
time of the attack. Simes later established that the plate matched an unmarked
police car — an allegation of direct police involvement in an anti-gay attack.
Rosendale and Simes met for the first time in 2013 after Simes published his
account. This allegation makes the Rosendale case exceptional in the documented
history: it moves beyond systemic investigative failure into potential active
misconduct.

Rosendale has spoken publicly about his experience and has been an advocate for
accountability. His 2018 ABC News article (with Paul Simes) is rated
primary-source-quality.
