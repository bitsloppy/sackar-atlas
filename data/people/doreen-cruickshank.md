Detective Sergeant Doreen Cruickshank was the Officer in Charge of the original
investigation into the death of Scott Johnson at Manly Police in 1988–1989.

At the first coronial inquest (16 March 1989), she gave evidence that the clifftop
area at Blue Fish Point, North Head, was not known to NSW Police as a gay beat or
meeting place — basing this assessment solely on the absence of any reported
violence there.

The Sackar Inquiry noted that evidence before the Inquiry indicated the area had
in fact been well-known as a beat from at least the 1970s. The original investigation
was conducted as a suspected suicide, not a homicide inquiry.
