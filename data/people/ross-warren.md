Ross Bradley Warren was twenty-five years old, a television presenter in Wollongong,
and openly gay with family, friends, and his employer. He was described by those
who knew him as extremely friendly and good-natured, with a wicked sense of humour,
and driven in his career. He had been reading the weather on WIN4 Television's
6pm news.

He disappeared in the early hours of Saturday 22 July 1989 near Marks Park in
Tamarama — a well-known gay beat on the cliff path between Bondi and Tamarama.
His car and keys were found at the park. His body has never been found.

In 2005, Deputy State Coroner Milledge found that he was a victim of homicide by
person or persons unknown. The Sackar Inquiry confirmed this finding in 2023.

He had family — parents and siblings who knew and loved him. His mother wrote
letters to police throughout the 1990s seeking answers. No one has ever been
charged with his murder.
