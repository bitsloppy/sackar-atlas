Jacques Paul Musy was the long-term partner of Gilles Mattaini. They met in Paris
in about 1978 and moved to Sydney together in 1983. When Mattaini disappeared on
15 September 1985, Musy was visiting family in France, having left Sydney on
16 August 1985.

Musy gave a statement to Operation Taradale on 3 August 2002 — seventeen years
after Gilles disappeared. He described Mattaini as shy and private, happy in their
relationship, not a user of the Marks Park beat, and looking forward to both Musy's
return and a visit from their French friend Antony Wyszynski. He believed Mattaini
had been happy and showed no suicidal tendencies since they had met in 1978.

His evidence was central to the Milledge Inquest and the Sackar Inquiry's
understanding of who Gilles Mattaini was and what his state of mind had been.
