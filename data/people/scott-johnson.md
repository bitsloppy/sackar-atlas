Scott Russell Johnson was an American mathematician completing his doctorate at
the Australian National University. He was twenty-seven years old when he died.
He had a partner. He was gay.

His body was found at the foot of the cliffs at Blue Fish Point, North Head, Manly,
on the morning of 10 December 1988. He was naked. A folded pile of his clothing
was at the top of the cliff.

Three coronial inquests over twenty-nine years considered his death. The third, in
2017, found he had been killed by persons who attacked him because they perceived
him to be homosexual.

In 2023, Scott White was convicted of his manslaughter.

The Australian National University posthumously awarded Scott Johnson his doctorate
in 1995, seven years after his death.
