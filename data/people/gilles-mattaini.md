Gilles Jacques Mattaini was born in France on 25 October 1958. He met his partner
Jacques Musy in Paris in about 1978; they were together for seven years before
moving to Sydney in 1983. He worked casually as a barman at the Menzies Hotel near
Wynyard and enjoyed walking the coastal path from Bondi to Bronte.

He disappeared on 15 September 1985 while his partner was visiting family in France.
His body has never been found. Nobody reported his disappearance to police in 1985
and no investigation took place until Operation Taradale in 2000 — fifteen years
later.

The Sackar Inquiry (2023) could not determine the cause or manner of his death, but
found objectively reason to suspect both that he was murdered and that LGBTIQ bias
was a factor. He was a gay man walking regularly near Marks Park — a well-known beat
and, by the mid-1980s, a known target area for anti-gay violence.

His mother Renée Mattaini died in 2017 still not knowing what had happened to her son.
