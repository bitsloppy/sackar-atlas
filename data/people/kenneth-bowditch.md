Detective Sergeant Kenneth Bowditch was the Officer in Charge at Paddington Police
responsible for the investigation into the disappearance of Ross Warren in July 1989.

The Sackar Inquiry found his so-called investigation constituted active misconduct.
He effectively closed the case within four days of Warren's disappearance and made
multiple false claims about investigative steps he stated he had taken — including
submitting a brief to the Coroner (never received), taking statements from Warren's
friends Craig Ellis and Paul Saucis (both denied this occurred), and involving the
Air Wing and Water Police (no records supported this).

Coroner Milledge described the investigation as "a grossly inadequate and shameful
investigation. Indeed, to characterise it as an investigation is to give it a label
it does not deserve."

The NSWPF accepted in submissions that Bowditch's "apparently false assertions"
were "extraordinary."
