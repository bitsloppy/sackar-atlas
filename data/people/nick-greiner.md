Nicholas Frank Greiner (born 27 April 1947) served as Premier of New South Wales from March 1988 to June 1992. His significance to this project predates his premiership: as Liberal Leader of the Opposition, Greiner seconded Neville Wran's private members bill to decriminalise male homosexual activity in NSW in early 1984, enabling it to pass on a conscience vote.

That act of cross-party support was politically notable. The ACON report *In Pursuit of Truth and Justice* (2018) and Garry Wotherspoon's historical analysis both document the difficulty of achieving decriminalisation in the face of sustained parliamentary resistance; it had taken nearly two decades after Britain's reform (1967) to reach NSW. Greiner's willingness to cross party lines on the matter helped secure a majority and ended the legislative criminalisation of homosexual activity between men.

As Premier from 1988, Greiner's tenure coincided with the continuation of the peak violence period documented in this project (1984–1991), the AIDS epidemic, and the early years of the Gay and Lesbian Liaison Officer (GLLO) program established under his predecessor's era. His premiership also preceded the Wood Royal Commission into police corruption, which exposed the systemic failures that shaped police culture during this period.

Note on institutional role: Greiner's primary relevance to this project is his role as Leader of the Opposition (1983–1988) when he seconded decriminalisation, but he is recorded here in his Premier role, which was his most senior position.
