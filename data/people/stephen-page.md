Detective Sergeant Stephen Page was the officer who led Operation Taradale — the
large-scale police reinvestigation into the suspected deaths of Ross Warren and
Gilles Mattaini, and the death of John Russell, conducted between 2000 and 2003.

Page came to the cases in 2000 after Ross Warren's mother's repeated letters to
police seeking answers about her son's disappearance finally reached him. His
enquiries, and the investigative work of Operation Taradale, assembled the brief
of evidence that underpinned the Milledge Inquest (2003–2005) — at which
Deputy State Coroner Milledge found Ross Warren was a victim of homicide and
John Russell had been thrown from the cliffs by person or persons unknown.

After retiring from the NSWPF, Page continued to assist families of victims of
gay hate crimes. In 2006, he was contacted by Steve Johnson (Scott Johnson's
brother), and drew Page's attention to similarities between the circumstances of
Scott Johnson's 1988 death and the Taradale cases. Page agreed the similarities
warranted reinvestigation, and assisted the Johnson family in preparing materials
for police and the State Coroner.
