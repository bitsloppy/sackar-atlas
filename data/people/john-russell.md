John Alan Russell was thirty-one years old when he was found dead at the base of
the Marks Park cliffs on the morning of 23 November 1989. He lived with his brother
Peter in Bondi, worked as a barman, and was described by his best friend as a happy,
easy-going person. He was gay, known to his brother and close friends. He had
recently inherited money and was planning to build a home on his father's farm at
Wollombi — everything was looking up.

Deputy State Coroner Milledge found in 2005 that he had been thrown from the cliff
onto rocks by a person or persons unknown. The Sackar Inquiry confirmed this in
2023. No one has ever been charged with his murder.

His brother Peter engaged with the Sackar Inquiry. Peter Russell learned through
the Inquiry that Strike Force Neiwand had internally reversed the Milledge homicide
finding — without ever telling the family.
