Cecil Abbott served as Commissioner of Police from December 1981 to August 1984. His tenure encompasses two watershed moments: the decriminalisation of male homosexual acts in New South Wales in January 1984, and the emergence of the AIDS crisis in Australia. While decriminalisation removed criminal penalties for consensual adult conduct, it did not alter policing culture, and the period saw escalating anti-gay violence concentrated around Oxford Street and inner-city Sydney. The AIDS crisis introduced a further layer of stigma and social hostility that was reflected in contemporaneous policing attitudes.
